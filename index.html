Overview and how to use this module

What this role does, how the training is organized, and the tracking format.

Who this training is for

This module is for a new team member who supports a provider during telemedicine visits at The Care Clinic (TCC). Day-to-day responsibilities include:

  • Virtual patient check-in via Doxy.
  • Clinic verification and insurance eligibility checks.
  • Keeping charts accurate in Charm EMR before the visit.
  • Joining 3-way Doxy calls with the provider and the patient.
  • Assisting the provider in finishing notes in Charm.
  • Handling checkout, including payment collection and follow-up scheduling.
  • Managing recalls and follow-ups.
  • Scheduling new and returning patients.

Format (matches the TCC Billing Training program)

This curriculum mirrors the structure used in the TCC Billing Training program in ClickUp: five tracks, thirteen modules, and every module uses the same layout.

Every module contains:
  • Why this module matters
  • Primary SOPs (with links and citations)
  • Prerequisites
  • Learning objectives
  • Step-by-step process
  • Required checks
  • Trainee checklist
  • Common errors
  • Practice items
  • A short quiz with answer validation
Source transparency. Verbatim scripts and numeric rules in this module come from TCC's internal documents in SharePoint and ClickUp (see the Sources section at the bottom). Any Charm EMR or Doxy workflow that is not directly documented in those sources is labeled "General platform guidance, verify locally" so trainees learn to confirm details with their supervisor before assuming.

Module 1: Role Overview and The Care Clinic ~45 min

Track 1 • Foundations • Module 1 of 13

Why this module matters

The telemedicine assistant sits between the patient and the provider. Getting the first interaction right, the handoff to the provider during the visit, and the closeout after the visit are the three places where mistakes become expensive (missed charges, compliance issues, or a patient who does not return). This module establishes the scope of the role and the expected standard of care.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts (SharePoint, TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25.pdf) - answering, verification, and check-in scripts.
  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint, Protocol for MAs.docx) - see Section 3 Telemedicine Visits and Recalls/Followups.
  • TCC Provider Orientation (SharePoint, TCC Provider Orientation (copy).pdf) - context on provider workflow in Charm.

Prerequisites

  • Active TCC email and building access (or remote credentials).
  • HIPAA training completed and documented in HR.

Learning objectives

  1. State the eight core responsibilities of the telemedicine assistant role.
  2. Identify which staff member owns which part of the visit (check-in, clinical, checkout).
  3. Explain why a clean check-in reduces errors later in billing and scheduling.

Step-by-step orientation

  1. Read Sections 2 and 3 of Protocol for MAs (In-Person Visits and Telemedicine Visits).
  2. Shadow one full day of telemedicine visits with an experienced assistant.
  3. Review the TCC organizational chart with your supervisor and confirm the chain of command for clinical questions, billing questions, and scheduling questions.

Required checks

  • Confirm you know who the on-call provider is each day.
  • Confirm you have the correct Doxy room URL for each provider you will assist.

Trainee checklist

  • HIPAA training complete.
  • Doxy room link saved and tested.
  • Charm EMR login verified.
  • SharePoint access to Front Desk and Provider folders verified.
  • Observed a full day of telemedicine visits.

Common errors

  • Treating the role as purely clerical. Clinical attention during the visit is part of the job (for example, noticing when a patient drops off the Doxy call or when audio fails).
  • Skipping the shadow day. Without it, checkout and recall workflows are much harder to learn.

Practice items

  • Draw the patient's journey from first phone call to follow-up scheduling on a whiteboard. Identify each step you own.

Module 3: HIPAA Verification and Phone Answering ~60 min

Track 2 • Patient Check-In and Clinic Verification • Module 3 of 13

Why this module matters

Every call, every check-in, and every patient interaction begins with identity verification. Releasing any protected health information without verification is a HIPAA violation. These scripts are not suggestions, they are mandatory.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - Answering Call Script, Second Call / Call Waiting Script, Hold Script, Transfer Script.
  • HIPAA Verification_staff (SharePoint, HIPAA Verification_staff.pdf).

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 and 2 complete.
  • HIPAA training on file with HR.

Learning objectives

  1. Recite the five-point HIPAA verification verbatim.
  2. Handle call waiting without disclosing information to the wrong party.
  3. Use the correct hold, return, and transfer scripts.

Five-point HIPAA verification (from TCC script)

Before you share any information about the patient or their care, you must verify:

  1. Full name.
  2. Date of birth.
  3. Address.
  4. Phone number.
  5. Emergency contact.
Rule. If any of the five items do not match the chart, stop, do not release information, and follow the escalation path to your supervisor. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25, HIPAA Verification section.)

Key scripts (verbatim)

  • Second call / call waiting: politely ask the first caller to hold, answer the second, get enough information to return the second caller, then go back to the first.
  • Returning from hold: thank the caller for holding before continuing.
  • Before transferring: tell the caller who you are transferring them to and why. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25.)

Step-by-step process for an incoming call

  1. Answer with the verbatim script.
  2. Ask for the patient's full name.
  3. Run the five-point HIPAA check.
  4. If verified, continue with the caller's request.
  5. If not verified, do not share PHI. Ask them to confirm details, or escalate.
  6. If you must put the caller on hold, use the hold script. When you return, thank them for holding.
  7. If you transfer, use the transfer script and warm-transfer when possible.

Required checks

  • You can recite all five verification items without looking.
  • You know the escalation path when verification fails.

Trainee checklist

  • HIPAA script memorized.
  • Role-played three incoming calls with the supervisor (one verified, one failed, one transfer).
  • Hold and transfer scripts used correctly in role play.

Common errors

  • Confirming information the caller gives you instead of asking for it (for example, reading the DOB out loud and asking "is that right?"). The caller must provide it.
  • Answering call waiting with a full greeting that accidentally includes PHI of the first caller.
  • Forgetting to thank the caller for holding.

Practice items

  • Role-play with your supervisor: one caller who passes verification, one who fails, one call-waiting scenario, one transfer.

Module 4: Doxy Virtual Check-In ~75 min

Track 2 • Patient Check-In and Clinic Verification • Module 4 of 13

Why this module matters

You are the first person a telemedicine patient sees. The Doxy check-in is where we run HIPAA verification, confirm the patient's physical location, confirm updated personal and insurance info, and enforce the 15-minute early window.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - Check-in Script for Scheduled Patients and related sections on privacy and location.
  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Section 3 Telemedicine Visits.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 3 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Run the full Doxy check-in script without prompts.
  2. Confirm the patient is physically located in a state where the provider is licensed.
  3. Capture any address, pharmacy, or insurance changes accurately.
  4. Enforce the 15-minute early arrival rule consistently.

Step-by-step check-in (from TCC script)

  1. Greet the patient in the Doxy room with the standard greeting.
  2. Run the five-point HIPAA verification (Module 3).
  3. Confirm the patient's current physical location, specifically the state. Document it.
  4. Ask about changes since the last visit: address, pharmacy, insurance.
  5. Confirm the patient is in a quiet, private location suitable for a medical visit.
  6. Remind the patient that the clinic expects check-in up to 15 minutes early. If they are more than late, follow the late-cancellation rules (Module 9).
  7. Update the chart in Charm with any changes before the provider joins.
Source. Location, changes-since-last-visit, privacy, and the 15-minute early window are all codified in TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25, Check-in Script for Scheduled Patients.

Required checks

  • Patient's physical location (state) is documented in the visit note.
  • Pharmacy, address, and insurance reviewed and corrected if needed.
  • Patient is in a private location. If not, offer to reschedule.

Trainee checklist

  • Location-in-state confirmed.
  • Pharmacy on file matches what patient reports.
  • Insurance card on file (front and back) still current.
  • Emergency contact still current.
  • Quiet, private location confirmed.

Common errors

  • Assuming the patient is in their home state. Patients travel.
  • Accepting "nothing has changed" without asking each item.
  • Admitting the patient to the provider before updating the chart.

Practice items

  • Role-play a check-in where the patient has moved to another state. Decide whether the provider can see them today.
  • Role-play a check-in where the patient is in a crowded coffee shop.

Module 5: Clinic Verification, Eligibility, and Chart Accuracy ~90 min

Track 2 • Patient Check-In and Clinic Verification • Module 5 of 13

Why this module matters

"Clinic verification" means confirming that TCC is the correct clinic for this visit (right provider, right license state, right services) and that the patient's insurance will cover the visit. Skipping this creates denied claims, mid-visit disruption, and unhappy patients.

Primary SOPs

  • ClickUp "TCC Billing SOP" document - Clinic Verification and Chart Setup module (used as the format template for this role's verification workflow).
  • ClickUp SOPs: Clinic Verification, Verifying Clinic, Creating New Chart, Phone + Fax (verify current URLs with your supervisor).
  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Section 3 Telemedicine Visits.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 4 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Perform a pre-visit clinic verification for a scheduled telemedicine patient.
  2. Run an eligibility check for the patient's insurance.
  3. Update Charm chart fields (demographics, insurance, pharmacy, emergency contact) accurately before the provider joins.
  4. Recognize when a visit must be rescheduled for out-of-state or coverage reasons.

Step-by-step

  1. Confirm the provider-patient pairing. Right provider, right license state for today's physical location.
  2. Confirm the service. Telemedicine visit type matches what is on the schedule.
  3. Eligibility check. Run the payer's eligibility check. Confirm coverage is active and the plan covers telemedicine for today's service. (General platform guidance, verify the exact tool used in Charm/clearinghouse with your supervisor.)
  4. Chart cleanup. In Charm, verify and update: demographics, insurance (primary and secondary), pharmacy, allergies, current medications, emergency contact, problem list, last note. (General platform guidance, verify locally.)
  5. Flag issues early. If eligibility fails, follow the self-pay script (Module 9) or reschedule.

Required checks

  • Insurance active today, for this plan, for telemedicine.
  • Patient's physical state matches provider's license.
  • Pharmacy and emergency contact verified this visit.

Trainee checklist

  • Eligibility response saved to the chart or accessible for billing.
  • Copay or self-pay amount known before the provider joins.
  • Any chart corrections made and saved.
  • Any red flags communicated to the provider in the Doxy chat or a Charm message before the visit starts.

Common errors

  • Running eligibility on the secondary only and missing primary termination.
  • Assuming the patient's insurance covers telemedicine. Some plans do not.
  • Skipping chart cleanup because "the provider will fix it." They often will not.

Practice items

  • Walk through a live pre-visit verification for two patients with your supervisor observing.
  • Find and read the ClickUp "Creating New Chart" SOP and explain the steps back.

Module 6: 3-Way Doxy Calls and Provider Handoff ~60 min

Track 3 • The Telemedicine Visit • Module 6 of 13

Why this module matters

Once you have completed check-in, you either transfer the patient to the provider or join the provider in a 3-way Doxy call. The handoff must be seamless and the patient should never feel handed off twice.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - Transfer Script, Unscheduled Doxy Login Script.
  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Section 3 Telemedicine Visits.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 5 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Execute a scheduled transfer using the verbatim transfer script.
  2. Join a 3-way Doxy call without disrupting the visit.
  3. Handle an unscheduled patient who logs in to Doxy using the correct script.

Step-by-step

  1. Finish check-in tasks from Module 4 and 5.
  2. Tell the patient you are now going to bring in the provider or transfer them. Use the transfer script.
  3. In Doxy, move the patient to the provider's room or invite the provider into your room, per clinic setup.
  4. If you are staying on a 3-way call, mute yourself unless addressed or typing in Charm.
  5. Drop off the call only when the provider indicates you can, and confirm you will return for checkout.

Unscheduled Doxy login

Unscheduled patients who log into Doxy follow a specific script. Use it verbatim. Do not ignore the waiting room. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25, Unscheduled Doxy Login Script.)

Required checks

  • Provider is aware of any issues found in check-in (chart, insurance, location, mood).
  • Patient knows what to expect next.
  • If unscheduled: the on-demand fee has been addressed before the provider joins (see Module 12).

Trainee checklist

  • Transfer script delivered word for word in at least one observed visit.
  • 3-way Doxy mechanics observed with an experienced assistant.
  • Unscheduled login script memorized.

Common errors

  • Silent handoff. The patient does not know you are leaving and the provider does not know the patient is incoming.
  • Forgetting to mute on a 3-way call.
  • Chatting in Doxy during the clinical portion of the visit.

Practice items

  • Observe three handoffs with an experienced assistant.
  • Complete one handoff yourself with the supervisor shadowing.

Module 7: Real-Time Chart Support in Charm ~75 min

Track 3 • The Telemedicine Visit • Module 7 of 13

Why this module matters

During the visit, the provider is focused on the patient. You are focused on the chart. You should document vitals if reported, medication changes, allergy changes, and any administrative items the provider calls out, so that finishing the note after the visit takes minutes, not hours.

Primary SOPs

  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Section 3 Telemedicine Visits.
  • TCC Provider Orientation (SharePoint) - Charm EMR tutorial references.
General platform guidance, verify locally. Charm's note templates and macro fields differ between provider accounts. The items below are the expected content, not the exact click path.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 6 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Type into Charm without disrupting the visit flow.
  2. Capture medication and allergy updates the moment the provider discusses them.
  3. Know what the provider will finish themselves (assessment, plan, prescriptions) versus what you will finish (demographics, pharmacy, follow-up date).

Step-by-step during the visit

  1. Keep the patient's chart open and on the correct visit note.
  2. Document any verbal updates the patient gives about medications, allergies, pharmacy, or contact information.
  3. When the provider verbalizes next steps (labs, UDS, follow-up window), record them so checkout is fast.
  4. Do not document clinical assessment language or diagnoses. That is the provider's job.

Required checks

  • Medication list reflects changes confirmed during the visit.
  • Pharmacy on file matches what the patient wants prescriptions sent to.
  • Any labs or UDS the provider ordered are listed for you to send at checkout.

Trainee checklist

  • Can type into Charm without looking at the keyboard.
  • Knows the scope: administrative and patient-reported data, not clinical opinion.
  • Has a system (a scratchpad or Charm messaging) for flagging items to the provider.

Common errors

  • Writing clinical language that should come from the provider.
  • Waiting until after the visit to capture updates. Details get lost.
  • Correcting the patient mid-visit. Note it and raise it with the provider.

Practice items

  • Shadow a provider for three visits. After each, list what you documented in real time.

Module 8: Helping Finish Notes ~45 min

Track 3 • The Telemedicine Visit • Module 8 of 13

Why this module matters

After the clinical portion ends, the provider may delegate administrative note-finishing tasks: setting the follow-up interval, ordering a UDS or lab, routing a referral, attaching a form. Doing this while the visit is fresh keeps charts clean and billing-ready.

Primary SOPs

  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Recalls/Followups section.
  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - UDS/Lab orders section.

  2. Transcribe the provider's follow-up interval into a recall.
  3. Send UDS home kit orders or lab orders per the correct window.

Step-by-step

  1. After the provider signs off clinically, ask: "Anything to finish on the chart while the patient is still here?"

Required checks

  • Recall date is set in Charm.
  • Orders are routed or queued.

Trainee checklist

  • Recalls entered on time.
  • Orders sent within the correct window.
  • No notes closed without checkout complete.

Common errors

  • Closing a note before checkout is done.
  • Missing the 7-day lab window and letting the order lapse.

Practice items

  • Complete three assistant-side note closeouts with supervisor review.

Module 9: Checkout Workflow and Payment Handling ~75 min

Track 4 • Checkout and Post-Visit • Module 9 of 13

Why this module matters

Checkout is where we collect payment, gather patient feedback, and book the follow-up. If you miss a payment here, recovering it later is painful. If you miss scheduling, the recall loop breaks.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - Payment Scripts (Insurance-S, Self-Pay copay, Insurance-A / Alma hold), Check-out Script.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 8 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Identify which payment script applies based on the patient's coverage.
  2. Deliver the full checkout script including feedback, follow-up scheduling, and cancellation policy.
  3. Explain the $50 late cancellation fee correctly.

Which payment script to use

  • Insurance-S (Standard) / Self-Pay copay. Copay collected at checkout.
  • Insurance-A / Alma hold. Authorization hold script, not a direct collection.

Use the script for the category the patient is in. Do not improvise. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25, Payment Scripts.)

Step-by-step checkout

  1. Thank the patient and confirm the visit is over.
  2. Run the appropriate payment script.
  3. Ask for feedback on the visit.
  4. Schedule the follow-up using the recall date the provider set (Module 10).
  5. Remind the patient of the $50 late cancellation fee, per the TCC check-out script.
  6. Confirm contact preferences for appointment reminders.
  7. Close the chart administrative fields so the provider can sign.
Verbatim rule. The $50 late cancellation fee is stated in the TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts. Deliver the script word for word; do not soften it, and do not waive the fee without supervisor approval.

Required checks

  • Payment captured or Alma-style hold placed.
  • Follow-up scheduled on the recall date.
  • Cancellation fee explained out loud.

Trainee checklist

  • Knows the three payment scripts and when each applies.
  • Delivered a full checkout in role play without prompts.
  • Can state the $50 late cancellation fee without hedging.

Common errors

  • Dropping feedback and cancellation fee from the script.
  • Mixing Alma-hold language with Self-Pay copay language.
  • Booking the follow-up outside the provider's recall window.

Practice items

  • Run three role-play checkouts covering: Self-Pay copay, Insurance-S with copay, Insurance-A / Alma hold.

Module 10: Follow-Ups and Recalls ~60 min

Track 4 • Checkout and Post-Visit • Module 10 of 13

Why this module matters

A recall is the clinic's promise that a patient will come back at the right clinical interval. When recalls are missed, continuity of care suffers, prescriptions lapse, and revenue is lost. This is one of the most visible measures of the telemedicine assistant's performance.

Primary SOPs

  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Recalls/Followups section.
  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - Check-out Script.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 9 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Translate the provider's spoken follow-up interval ("see me in 4 weeks") into a recall date in Charm.
  2. Run a weekly recall audit to catch upcoming follow-ups and past-due patients.
  3. Follow the outreach cadence your supervisor sets for past-due recalls.

Step-by-step

  1. At checkout, confirm the recall interval from the provider (for example, 2, 4, 6, or 12 weeks).
  2. Create the recall in Charm with the correct date.
  3. Attempt to schedule the follow-up now. If the patient cannot commit, send them options by their preferred contact method.
  4. Weekly, pull the recall report and act on upcoming and past-due items per the Protocol for MAs.

Required checks

  • Recall exists in Charm for every telemedicine visit you closed this week.
  • No recall is more than 30 days past-due without an outreach attempt logged.

Trainee checklist

  • Recalls scheduled at checkout for every visit.
  • Weekly recall audit completed and documented.
  • Outreach logged for past-due patients.

Common errors

  • Typing a recall without a date.
  • Skipping the weekly audit.
  • Scheduling the follow-up outside the provider's interval because "that's the first open slot."

Practice items

  • Run a recall audit with supervisor oversight on your second week.

Module 11: UDS and Lab Orders ~45 min

Track 4 • Checkout and Post-Visit • Module 11 of 13

Why this module matters

Orders have tight time windows and the windows are documented in the TCC scripts. Missing a window means the patient has to repeat a conversation, the order must be reissued, and care is delayed.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - UDS/Lab Orders section.
  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - UDS/Lab Order section.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 10 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Explain and enforce the 2-week window for UDS home kit orders.
  2. Explain and enforce the 7-day window for lab orders.
  3. Use the correct patient-facing script when delivering lab or UDS instructions.

Step-by-step

  1. When the provider orders a UDS home kit, send it and communicate to the patient that they have 2 weeks to complete it.
  2. When the provider orders labs, send the order and tell the patient they have 7 days to complete it.
  3. Document the order and window in Charm.
  4. Follow up with any patient who misses a window.

Required checks

  • Patient can state back the window before hanging up.
  • Order is visible in the chart.
  • Follow-up outreach is scheduled if the window lapses.

Trainee checklist

  • Knows 7-day lab window.
  • Knows 2-week UDS home kit window.
  • Can deliver the patient-facing instructions word for word.

Module 12: Scheduling (New, Follow-Up, On-Demand, Cancellations) ~75 min

Track 5 • Scheduling, Communication, and Special Cases • Module 12 of 13

Why this module matters

Scheduling is where mistakes in earlier steps become visible. A bad eligibility check shows up as a denied claim. A missed recall shows up as a no-show. Great scheduling makes the rest of the clinic function.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - On-demand script, Check-in script for scheduled patients, Check-out script.
  • Protocol for MAs (SharePoint) - Section 3 Telemedicine Visits.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 11 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Schedule a new patient correctly, including intake and insurance on file.
  2. Schedule a follow-up at the provider's recall interval.
  3. Apply the $25 on-demand fee correctly.
  4. Apply the $50 late cancellation fee correctly.

Step-by-step: new patient

  1. Capture full name, DOB, address, phone, emergency contact, pharmacy.
  2. Confirm physical location and state.
  3. Capture insurance (front and back of card) and run eligibility.
  4. Pair patient with a provider licensed in that state.
  5. Book the intake/telemedicine slot.
  6. Send any pre-visit paperwork.

Step-by-step: follow-up

  1. Use the recall date set at the last visit.
  2. Offer at least two time options within the recall window.
  3. Book in Charm and confirm the reminder channel.

Step-by-step: on-demand

  1. Use the on-demand script verbatim.
  2. Collect the $25 on-demand fee before the provider joins. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25.)
  3. Run abbreviated check-in.

Step-by-step: cancellations

  1. Offer reschedule first.
  2. If the cancellation is within the late window, apply the $50 late cancellation fee per the TCC script.
  3. Document the cancellation reason.

Required checks

  • No visit is booked without eligibility or self-pay path.
  • No visit is booked to a provider who is not licensed in the patient's physical state.
  • Fees are applied per script, not per patient preference.

Trainee checklist

  • Completed one new patient schedule with supervisor oversight.
  • Completed one follow-up schedule.
  • Completed one on-demand intake with $25 fee collected.
  • Completed one cancellation with $50 fee applied per script.

Common errors

  • Booking a telemedicine visit for a patient traveling in a non-license state without flagging it.
  • Waiving the $25 or $50 fee without supervisor approval.
  • Scheduling outside the provider's recall window.

Practice items

  • Role-play four scenarios: new patient, returning follow-up, on-demand, late cancellation.

Module 13: Medication Messages, Crisis, and Special Scripts ~60 min

Track 5 • Scheduling, Communication, and Special Cases • Module 13 of 13

Why this module matters

Two kinds of calls need care beyond the standard scripts: medication messages and crisis calls. The TCC scripts set both the language and the escalation expectations. A zero-tolerance policy applies to harassment.

Primary SOPs

  • TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25 (SharePoint) - Medication message scripts, Crisis resources, Zero-tolerance harassment policy.

Prerequisites

  • Modules 1 through 12 complete.

Learning objectives

  1. Deliver medication message scripts verbatim.
  2. Recognize a crisis call and provide the correct resources (988 and Text "HELLO" to 741741).
  3. Apply the zero-tolerance policy consistently and escalate correctly.

Crisis resources (verbatim)

  • 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline. Dial or text 988.
  • Crisis Text Line. Text "HELLO" to 741741.

(Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25, Crisis resources.)

Rule. If a patient is in immediate danger, do not disconnect. Stay with them, connect them to 988, and escalate to the provider or supervisor immediately.

Medication messages

Follow the TCC medication message scripts for refills, questions, and side-effect reports. Do not give clinical advice. Route to the provider. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25, Medication message scripts.)

Zero-tolerance harassment policy

TCC has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment. You may and should disengage and escalate per the script if a patient or caller is abusive. (Source: TCC Doxy & Phone Scripts 4_25.)

Step-by-step for a potential crisis call

  1. Stay calm. Acknowledge the patient.
  2. Ask directly if they are safe right now.
  3. Provide 988 and 741741 resources verbatim.
  4. Do not end the call; bring in the provider or supervisor.
  5. Document the call thoroughly in Charm.

Required checks

  • You know the two crisis resources by memory.
  • You know how to escalate in real time to a provider or supervisor.
  • You know how to disengage per the zero-tolerance policy.

Trainee checklist

  • 988 and 741741 resources memorized.
  • Role-played a crisis call with supervisor.
  • Role-played a zero-tolerance escalation.

Common errors

  • Giving clinical advice during a medication message.
  • Hanging up on an abusive caller without escalation.
  • Providing crisis resources inconsistently.

Practice items

  • Three role-plays: refill question, crisis disclosure, abusive caller.
